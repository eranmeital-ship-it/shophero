# ShopHero Content Strategy

> Consulted for any blog / article / content task. The goal is store-aligned
> content that drives organic traffic AND sales — never generic filler. Always
> honor the Brand Kit (voice, audience, do/don'ts).

## 1. Analyze the store FIRST (don't write blind)
Before writing anything:
- Read the **existing blog posts/pages** (titles, topics, dates) and the **product catalog + collections**.
- Find the **gaps**: buying questions not answered, product categories with no supporting content, missing comparison/how-to/care/gift content, thin or outdated posts.
- Prioritize by **value to THIS store**: bottom-funnel/commercial intent first (buying guides, "best X for Y", comparisons, how-to-choose), then how-to/care, then top-of-funnel awareness.

Output a short prioritized **topic plan** (topic · primary keyword · search intent · the product/collection it links to) BEFORE writing.

## 2. Topic strategy
- **Pillar + cluster:** a pillar piece per main category, with cluster posts that link back to it.
- Match **search intent** (transactional / commercial / informational) to the funnel stage.
- **Tie every post to products**: link to the relevant PDPs and collections (this is how content earns revenue, not just traffic).
- **Seasonal/timely:** gift guides, holidays, launches, trends in the niche.
- **Variety:** alternate intent types and formats — never publish many near-identical posts.

## 3. Writing each post
- On-brand voice; original, genuinely helpful, specific to the niche (E-E-A-T). No fluff or generic AI padding.
- Structure: compelling H1, a hook intro, scannable H2/H3, short paragraphs, lists, and a clear **CTA linking to a product/collection**.
- **SEO:** one primary keyword + related terms; keyword in the title, H1, first ~100 words and meta description; descriptive meta description; image alt text; Article structured data if the theme supports it; a few internal links + 1–2 authoritative outbound links.
- Length to intent: buying guides ~1000–1500 words, how-tos ~700–1000.

## The 4 blog formats that actually rank & convert (use these, not lifestyle fluff)
Generic blogs ("5 fun facts", "how to stay cozy this winter") rarely rank or sell. These four target bottom/mid-funnel buyers, convert, earn backlinks, and get cited by AI:
1. **You vs Competitor** — "Brand A vs Brand B". Catches people comparing; mentioning the competitor's name can rank you for their branded search. Position yourself honestly as the stronger choice.
2. **Alternatives to Competitor** — "best alternatives to [competitor]". Catches people leaving a competitor; list options, put yourself first (truthfully).
3. **Best [product] for [use case]** — "best eye serums for women over 50". Clear buying intent + lots of low-competition long-tail.
4. **Buying guides** — evergreen "how to choose…/complete guide to…". Educates hesitant buyers and naturally earns backlinks/authority.
You only need ~10–15 well-targeted posts, not hundreds. **Before writing, check the SERP:** if blogs rank for the term, write the blog; if collection pages rank, build a collection instead.

## Finding topics (validated demand)
Use **Google autosuggest** + the "related searches" at the bottom of results, and tools like **answer the public** / Keyword Planner. Pick questions people actually search — informational queries ("how to / what / best / why") that map to your products.

## Internal linking (make the content earn)
Every post links **into a money page** (key collection/product) within the **first 100–200 words**, plus **3–5 links** to related posts. Descriptive 2–4 word anchor text. This passes authority to the pages that convert and keeps readers moving toward purchase. (See seo_playbook for the full linking system.)

## 4. Editorial calendar (for content plans)
- Spread topics across the period; mix intent types; weave in seasonal posts.
- For an N-day plan, produce the full topic calendar up front (date · topic · keyword · linked product · intent), then write to it.

## Avoid
- Thin/duplicate content, keyword stuffing, publishing without internal links, off-brand voice, or topics unrelated to what the store actually sells.
