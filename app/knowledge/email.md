## Why email (the case)
Email is the **highest-ROI channel** (~$35–45 back per $1; ~25% open rate vs <1% engagement on social). It's an **owned channel** (no algorithm risk) with **direct access** to people who already opted in — you've already paid to acquire them, so email is just profitable follow-up. For active stores it routinely drives **~30–40% of revenue**.

## Cadence
Send at least **1 campaign/week** to start; ramp to **2–3/week** as the list tolerates it. Consistency trains subscribers to expect (and open) your emails. Flows run 24/7 in the background on top of campaigns.

## Two engines — set up BOTH
1. **Flows (automations)** — built once, run forever. Set these up FIRST.
2. **Campaigns (broadcasts)** — one-off sends to segments (sales, launches, news).

## Flows (automations), in priority order
1. **Welcome series (4 emails)** — the highest-engagement moment; nurture, then ask:
   1) **Welcome** — who they are, where they landed, why they signed up; deliver any signup offer.
   2) **About us / brand story** — why they should keep listening.
   3) **Hero products / social proof** — your best products & proof (alternate with "follow us" social).
   4) **The offer** — conditional split: if they've purchased, skip the discount; if not, send it.
   Philosophy: *don't propose on the first date* — earn trust across 1–3 emails, then ask for the sale.
2. **Abandoned cart** (added to cart, no checkout) — sooner is better: email 1 ~1h, email 2 ~12h, email 3 ~24h **with a coupon**. Show the abandoned products dynamically. The single biggest flow for recovered revenue.
3. **Abandoned checkout** (reached checkout, didn't complete) — a daily follow-up sequence (reminder → social proof/objection → incentive).
4. **Browse / window-shopper** (visited or viewed a product, no add/buy) — "still thinking about X?"; for repeat lurkers, a stronger incentive (e.g. 20–25% off) to convert.
5. **Post-purchase** — thank-you + what's next → review request → cross-sell of complementary products.
6. **Win-back / inactive** (no engagement ~60–90 days) — a reason to return + incentive; otherwise sunset to protect deliverability.
7. **VIP / birthday** — reward best customers; early access, special offers.

## Campaigns — an idea bank (so "what do I send?" is never the blocker)
New arrivals · new collection · product teaser · sale / discount · upcoming sale ("save the date") · back in stock / restock · long-weekend or holiday · bundle spotlight · how-to / education · reviews & UGC roundup · last chance / ends-tonight. Tie each to a real reason and a single offer.

## Segmentation (don't blast everyone)
Send the right email to the right group: **all subscribers**, **non-purchasers** (push the offer), **first-time vs repeat buyers** (loyalty/cross-sell), **active** (opened ≤30 days), **inactive** (no opens 60–90 days), **window shoppers** (viewed, never bought), **VIP**, **birthday**. Segmenting lifts opens and protects sender reputation.

## Writing each email
- **One goal, one primary CTA** — don't bury it.
- **Subject line** (the open lever): short (~≤50 chars), action + emotion words, balanced word/character count, no spammy words/ALL CAPS. A/B test; grade it before sending.
- **Preview text** complements (doesn't repeat) the subject; **personalize** ("Hi {first name}", default "friend") — lifts opens.
- **Text↔image balance**: real text (not an all-image email) + images + clear CTAs — for both deliverability and appeal.
- **Mobile-first** — most opens are on mobile; always preview on phone.
- **Discounts**: state the value clearly and add an **expiration** (7–14 days) for honest urgency.

## Deliverability (stay out of spam)
- **Send from your authenticated domain**, never a personal/free address.
- Balanced text/image, no spam-trigger words/ALL CAPS, keep the list clean (sunset inactives), segment instead of blasting.

## Grow the list
Footer **signup form** + a dedicated signup section; offer a **first-order discount** (or free gift) to capture the email. The flows above then convert new subscribers automatically.

## Tools (advise — ShopHero can't send)
**Shopify Email** (built-in, cheapest, fine to start) for basic campaigns + simple automations; **Klaviyo** or **Omnisend** for serious segmentation + pre-built flows (both have generous free tiers). Discount codes generated in the ESP sync to Shopify checkout.
