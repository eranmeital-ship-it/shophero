## Mindset & the metrics that matter
- Most stores convert at **1–3%** (avg ~1.4%); the **top 1% hit 6–10%**. Same traffic at 2× the conversion rate = 2× sales at zero extra ad cost. 4×ing CRO can take a brand from $500K → $2M with the *same* traffic.
- **Conversion rate alone is a vanity metric.** Track the chain: CR → **AOV** (avg order value) → **RPV** (revenue/visitor) → **PPV** (profit/visitor). Optimize **RPV/PPV**, not raw CR. A change that lifts CR but drops AOV/profit is a loss. Think **LTV**, not just this month.
- If a change doesn't move RPV or PPV, it's noise.

## #1 lever: the OFFER (fix this first)
The offer is the highest-leverage thing on the whole site — it's what moves a brand from $500K to $2M. "Offer" = the **full package**, not just the product: price, bundles, upsells, guarantee, shipping, and how the value is positioned.
- **Don't copy competitors' offers.** Build a unique one.
- **Frame a "hero offer" in first view** — stack the value visibly so it feels irresistible. e.g. *"86% off today · 30 servings · free creamer · free week · bonus gift."* Show all the deals at once, above the fold.

## Price testing (always be testing)
- Never assume your price is optimal. **Split-test ±$5** (sell at $29.99 → test $24.99 and $34.99). **$1 differences move real money.**
- Counterintuitive but common: **raising price often holds CR steady (or improves it)** → instant revenue lift *and* more profit (lower cost %, less CS/ops per order). Even if CR dips slightly, higher AOV usually wins on total profit.
- Decision rule: keep the variant with higher **RPV/PPV**, not higher CR.

## Price-chunking & the upsell stack (most stores miss this)
Don't try to take all the money at the product price. **Lower the entry price → far more add-to-carts → then stack margin back through the funnel.** People in a "yes loop" keep saying yes.
1. **Pre-checked checkbox upsell** on the PDP (~**70% take** — it's the path of least resistance) → +$4.99.
2. **Pre-purchase popup upsell** before checkout (~**50% take**) → +$9.99.
3. **Shipping** as its own line ($4.99–$9.99) where appropriate.
4. **Post-purchase upsells/downsells** on the thank-you page — pure margin, CAC already paid.

Net effect: similar AOV to a high sticker price, but **many more orders**. Always A/B this against taking the money upfront — sometimes upfront wins, but you won't know without the test.

## Bundles & variants
- **Multipacks** with a small discount ("Buy 2, save 15%") for anything consumable/repeat.
- **Premium / deluxe variants** of the same product on the same page — a small added feature you can upcharge $10–$20.
- **Decoy / good-better-best pricing:** people distrust a blunt "50% off," but if there are 5 variants where 4 cost ~$100 and one is $50, they trust it and feel smart taking the "deal" (which you engineered).
- **Subscribe & save** (recurring) and **quantity order bumps** lift AOV and LTV.

## Trust & objection handling (huge lever)
- **Reviews are the strongest proof** — more persuasive than your own copy. Surface rating + count high on PDP and collection cards.
- **The objection-reversal review framework:** *"At first I thought **X**, but actually **Y**."* (e.g. *"I thought the bands would rip, but they've lasted months."*) These act like pre-emptive FAQs from real people.
- **Mine objections** from competitor **1-star reviews** (Amazon), **TikTok comments**, **Reddit**. Your buyers were likely burned by similar products. Bake those objections (reversed) into product descriptions, ad copy, and pinned comments.
- **Where objection-handling lands hardest:** reviews > the PDP body (reviews feel more honest) — and even stronger: ad **comment sections**, **Instagram story highlights**, **top comments** on Facebook ads.
- **Policies as trust signals — on the PDP, under add-to-cart**, not buried in the footer: "Free returns within 30 days," "Ships in 3–5 business days." Visible policy reduces perceived risk → higher RPV.
- **UGC can lift CR ~30%.** Unpolished customer photos, TikTok reactions, raw videos — they feel real and answer "do people actually use this?"
- **Credibility callouts:** real press logos ("as seen in"), hard stats ("6M orders delivered," "93.6% love the taste," "10,000+ studies"), "dermatologist recommended," guarantees.
- **Layer trust across the whole journey.** Trust is decided in ~**0.05s** of landing. Branding must be **consistent ad → homepage → PDP → cart** (and Instagram/story highlights too) — inconsistency reads as a scam and they bounce.

## Site speed
- **Every 1s of load delay ≈ 7% fewer conversions.** The #1 speed killer is large unoptimized images.
- Keep imagery high-res (it converts) but **compress + lazy-load below the fold**. (Merchant tooling like TinyIMG automates compression.)

## Diagnose the funnel, don't guess
Don't look only at overall CR — read the **conversion breakdown** and fix the bottleneck stage:
- **Low add-to-cart ratio** → ATC button not visible / blends in / below the fold, too much friction, missing education (ingredients, sizing, benefits), or price too high.
- **High ATC but big drop to reach-checkout** → cart friction: a separate cart *page* instead of a drawer, missing payment badges, surprise shipping, discount didn't apply.
- **Reaches checkout but doesn't complete** → checkout issues: shipping thresholds/cost, too few payment methods, weak trust/review badges, slow shipping time.

## Homepage
One job: **clarity, value, direction** — not a lookbook. Hero formula:
- **Headline** = what you offer (the outcome). **Subheadline** = why it matters / for whom.
- **Product front & center**, one obvious **CTA** ("Shop now"), and an **offer banner** (free shipping / sale / bundle). New brand? State plainly what you sell and the problem you solve.

## Product page (PDP) — the money page
Three jobs at once: **inform, persuade, drive action.**
- **Above the fold:** title, price, key benefit, hero image, add-to-cart, trust signals — don't make them scroll to buy.
- **Sticky add-to-cart** on scroll (especially mobile). High-contrast button (never white-on-white).
- **Swatches/size selectors** easy to choose; size guide; clear in-stock state.
- **Benefit-led bullets** in short skimmable lines; **iconography** to convey details without reading.
- **Accordions** for depth (shipping, returns, specs) so the page feels light and expands on demand.
- **Trust under the buy button:** returns, shipping ETA, guarantee, secure-payment.
- Reviews with photos; surface count. **Urgency/scarcity only when true** — fake urgency erodes trust.

## Cart & checkout (where sales die)
- **Use a slide-out / drawer cart — never a separate cart page** (the extra page is friction that kills conversions).
- Show cart contents + a **free-shipping progress bar** ("$12 away from free shipping").
- **All payment options:** Shop Pay, Apple/Google Pay, PayPal, Klarna/Afterpay. Accelerated checkout up top.
- **Auto-detect currency/language** for international (Shopify Markets) — don't make buyers do the math.
- Trust/review badges + shipping time at checkout. A "Continue" button (vs. "Checkout") keeps an upsell sequence flowing.

## Design — don't over-index early
- Design barely matters until ~$100K/mo **as long as it's not below ~5/10**. A scammy-looking site tanks CR to near zero; a clean "5/10" converts fine. **Offer ≫ design** early; polish later.
- The exception that always matters: **consistent branding** and the **first-load experience** (what they clicked in the ad is the first thing they see on the page).

## Testing discipline (the CRO flywheel)
- **Test ONE variable at a time** (price, headline, CTA, an upsell, a bundle) — never several at once, or you won't know what worked.
- Wins **compound multiplicatively**: +10% × +15% × +20% ≈ **2–3×**, not +45%. Small wins stack into a transformed store.
- **CRO is never finished** — markets shift, competitors copy, the same pattern loses novelty. Re-test.

## Proven buy-box & offer setups (from top-converting DTC stores)
The patterns the best DTC product pages share. Use ONLY true claims/numbers — never fabricate reviews, scarcity or press.
- **Buy box order:** title → rating + review count → benefit subhead → offer → variant/quantity/subscription selector → strikethrough price + “% OFF / you save $X” → big high-contrast CTA stating action + price → free-shipping/guarantee line → payment/security badges.
- **Offer model by product type:** consumables = **subscription-first** (Subscribe & Save default, escalating 20/25/30% off, "MOST POPULAR" on the middle tier); bundleable goods = **quantity tiers** (1/2/4/6/12, escalating % off, a pre-selected "BEST VALUE" tier, free shipping unlocked higher up, per-unit + total + savings shown); add **free-gift value stacking** and a **pre-checked order-bump add-on** where it fits.
- **Honest urgency:** real countdowns, "ships by [date]", "X left at this price", a truly out-of-stock tier — never faked.
- **Social proof:** big real numbers, rating+count by the title, verified reviews with photos, before/after, expert endorsement.
- **Trust:** money-back guarantee badge, secure-checkout + payment badges, free shipping/returns, real "AS SEEN ON" press, third-party testing, free-from icon row.
- **CTA & copy:** benefit/first-person CTA ("Yes, I want this!"), outcome headline, checkmark benefit bullets, objection handling inline.
For full PDP assembly, also use the page_kit.

- **Directly implementable now:** PDP/homepage layout & copy, hero formula, benefit bullets + icons, accordions, sticky add-to-cart, trust badges & policy lines under ATC, review/UGC sections, drawer cart (theme setting), bundle/variant *structure*, urgency *if true*, image lazy-loading, consistent branding.
- **Recommend (needs an app or merchant action), don't fake:** A/B price testing (Intelligems/Visually), one-click pre/post-purchase upsells (Zipify), subscriptions, payment-method setup, Shopify Markets currency.
